Lewis Carroll's *Alice in Wonderland* has been a recurring touchstone in Brian's work — the dreamlike logic, the characters who exist at the boundary between sense and nonsense, and the way the story gives permission to take the world completely sideways.

The visual tradition around Alice is rich, starting with John Tenniel's original illustrations and continuing through countless reinterpretations. Brian's engagement with the material isn't reverential — it's more like a conversation with the imagery that resurfaces in unexpected ways.
