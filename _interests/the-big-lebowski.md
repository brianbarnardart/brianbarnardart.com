*The Big Lebowski* (1998) occupies its own strange corner of cinema — a film that shouldn't hold together as well as it does, built on a deadpan absurdism that rewards repeated viewing. The Coen Brothers' meditation on bowling, rugs, and the philosophy of doing nothing particularly well has accumulated a genuine cult following and a rich visual identity.

The film's color palette, its mix of grandiosity and squalor, and the way it finds meaning in the meandering all resonate with Brian's sensibility.
